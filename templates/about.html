SodhiCable's competitive position depends on operational excellence — OEE >85%, scrap <3%, and on-time delivery >95%. The MES is the primary tool for achieving and sustaining these targets.

ISA-95 Functional Hierarchy

ANSI/ISA-95.00.01-2010 — Enterprise-Control System Integration. SodhiCable implements Levels 0–4.

Level 4 — Business Planning & Logistics
ERP, MRP/demand planning, sales order management, financial reporting, supply chain coordination
SodhiCable: /mrp · /sales · /executive · /suppliers
Level 3 — Manufacturing Operations Management (MOM/MES)
THIS IS WHERE THE MES OPERATES
MESA-11 Functions: F1 Resource Allocation, F2 Scheduling, F3 Dispatching, F4 Document Control, F5 Data Collection, F6 Labor, F7 Quality/SPC, F8 Process Mgmt, F9 Maintenance, F10 Traceability, F11 Performance Analysis
SodhiCable: /scheduling · /dispatch · /quality · /labor · /equipment · /traceability · /performance
Level 2 — Supervisory Control & Automated Control (SCADA / DCS / PLC)
PLC logic, DCS control loops, PID controllers, HMI operator screens, alarm management, batch sequencing
SodhiCable: /scada (SCADA drill-down) · /process (PID, CUSUM/EWMA, alarms)
Level 1 — Sensing & Actuation (Field Devices)
Sensors (thermocouples, laser micrometers, pressure transducers), actuators (heater SCRs, motor drives, valve positioners), spark testers, encoders
SodhiCable: /scada (sensor drill-down) · process_data_live, environmental_readings, spark_test_log
Level 0 — Physical Production Process
The actual physical and chemical transformations: compound mixing (Banbury), wire drawing, insulation extrusion, PLCV vulcanization, braiding, cabling, armoring, spark/hipot testing, cutting, packaging
SodhiCable: /des (DES simulation) · /simulation (8-stage factory model)

Data Flow Examples — How Information Crosses ISA-95 Boundaries

These worked examples show how events propagate through the automation hierarchy in a real production scenario.

Flow 1: Spark Test Failure Cascade

L1 Spark tester detects pinhole at 1,247 ft (3.2 kV)
↓ process_data_live INSERT
L2 Alarm triggered, line speed reduced, hold signal sent
↓ process_deviations INSERT, severity='Critical'
L3 F7: NCR auto-created → F8: Hold placed → F10: Lot quarantined
↓ shift_reports UPDATE, scrap_log INSERT
L4 Yield KPI decremented, scrap cost added, promise date re-evaluated

Flow 2: Temperature PID Control Loop

L1 Thermocouple reads 379°F (setpoint 365°F, error +14°F)
↓ process_data_live
L2 PID: u(t) = Kp·e + Ki·∫e + Kd·de/dt → heater reduced 85%→62%
↓ control output applied to actuator
L1 Thermocouple reads 371°F (converging toward setpoint)
↓ if |error| persists > 2σ
L3 CUSUM/EWMA flags drift → F8 alarm → F9 maintenance check

Flow 3: End-of-Shift Rollup

L1-2 480 min sensor data, 12 SPC samples, 3 spark tests collected
↓ aggregated per work center
L3 F11: OEE = 82.1% (A=91%, P=93%, Q=97%), FPY=96.8%, 2 NCRs
↓ shift_reports INSERT
L4 Executive dashboard updated, cost/KFT recalculated, delivery dates adjusted

Beyond the MESA-11 Framework

SodhiCable MES extends well beyond the traditional 11 MESA functions with advanced analytics, simulation, and decision-support modules.

1. Discrete Event Simulation

8-stage factory model with configurable arrival rates, queueing analytics (M/M/1, M/M/c, M/G/1), breakdown injection, and what-if scenario analysis using Server-Sent Events for real-time streaming.

/des · /simulation

2. Predictive Maintenance

Failure probability modeling P(fail) = 1 - e(-t/MTBF), cost-of-delay analysis, optimal PM interval calculation, and risk-based maintenance scheduling.

/predictive

3. Supply Chain Risk

Lead time variability analysis, single-source dependency detection, coverage days calculation, and risk scoring across the entire material supply base.

/supply-risk

4. Bottleneck Analysis

Kingman's G/G/c approximation for wait-time estimation, shifting bottleneck identification, what-if capacity planning, and utilization heat-maps.

/bottleneck

5. Material Requirements Planning

Multi-level BOM explosion with 7 lot-sizing methods: Lot-for-Lot (L4L), EOQ, POQ, Fixed Order Quantity (FOQ), Silver-Meal, Wagner-Whitin, and EPQ.

/mrp

6. Customer Order Management

Sales order tracking, Available-to-Promise (ATP) calculation, revenue analysis by customer and product family, and demand forecasting.

/sales

7. Supplier Scorecards

Vendor performance rating, lead time tracking, on-time delivery scores, quality metrics, and availability analysis across the supply base.

/suppliers

8. AI / ML Analytics Layer

Anomaly detection on process data, predictive quality scoring, prescriptive scheduling recommendations, and pattern recognition on SPC data.

/ai

9. SCADA Visualization

ISA-95 Level 1-2 drill-down, real-time sensor views, work-center status dashboards, environmental readings, and alarm management displays.

/process

10. Energy Management

kWh per KFT tracking per work center. Extrusion heaters consume 50-150 kW, draw motors 15-50 kW, compound mixers 75-200 kW. Energy cost typically represents 8-12% of COGS in wire & cable. Tracks consumption trends and identifies energy waste.

Implemented — /scada

11. Environmental & Sustainability

Scrap-as-waste tracking with copper recovery rate ~98%. Compound waste stream monitoring. Carbon footprint per KFT: Scope 1 (natural gas for curing ovens) and Scope 2 (electricity for extrusion). Supports ESG reporting requirements.

Partial — scrap tracking active, carbon accounting planned

12. Supply Chain Integration

Beyond passive vendor scoring: vendor-managed inventory (VMI) for copper rod with auto-replenishment at min level, consignment stock management for specialty compounds, EDI 856/810 integration for advance shipping notices and invoices.

Partial — supplier scorecards active, VMI/EDI planned

13. Cybersecurity / OT Security

ISA/IEC 62443 zones and conduits model. Network segmentation between IT (Level 4) and OT (Levels 0-3) via DMZ. Audit trail for all data modifications (implemented). Role-based access control for operator/supervisor/manager/executive views. Intrusion detection at Level 2-3 boundary.

Partial — audit trail active, RBAC planned

14. Digital Twin

DES simulation engine provides the foundation for a process digital twin. 8-stage factory model with configurable inter-arrival times, service distributions, and breakdown rates. Future: live synchronization between physical process and simulation for real-time what-if analysis ('If DRAW-1 breaks down now, what happens to the schedule?').

Partial — DES active, live sync planned — /simulation

MESA International expanded beyond the original 11 functions with their Strategic Initiatives model (2004+), recognizing that modern MES/MOM systems must address Lean Manufacturing, Quality Management, Product Lifecycle Management, Asset Performance Management, and Real-Time Enterprise integration. SodhiCable MES v4.0 incorporates elements of all five strategic initiatives.

Research Gap & Contribution

To our knowledge, no peer-reviewed publication presents a complete MESA-11 implementation reference design for the wire and cable manufacturing industry. Existing MES literature either covers individual functions (SPC, scheduling, OEE) in isolation or presents vendor-specific case studies without reproducible architecture.

SodhiCable MES v4.0 fills this gap by providing a fully functional, open-source, single-dependency implementation covering all 11 MESA functions plus 14 beyond-MESA capabilities, with domain-specific features for continuous-process cable manufacturing including spark test integration, reel genealogy, compound shelf-life tracking, and ISA-95 Level 0-4 visualization.
