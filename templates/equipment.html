Weibull / Exponential CDF — Failure Probability Over Time

What this shows: Each curve is the probability that a piece of equipment will fail before operating hour t. Steeper curves = less reliable equipment needing earlier PM. The green dashed line at 10% is the typical PM scheduling target — equipment should be serviced before reaching that threshold.
Weibull shape (beta): beta > 1 = wear-out failures (increasing hazard — schedule PM aggressively), beta = 1 = random failures (exponential), beta < 1 = infant mortality.
After a PM: The clock resets to t=0. A completed PM effectively moves the equipment back to the left side of its curve, restarting the failure probability from near zero. This is why PM interval = the t value where the curve crosses your acceptable risk threshold (e.g., 10%).
