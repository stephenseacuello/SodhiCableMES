# MES Literature Review -- Comprehensive Search Results

## Overview
Below is a thorough, organized literature review covering published academic papers, industry case studies, and key references on Manufacturing Execution Systems (MES). Papers are grouped by the eight requested topic areas. For each entry, I provide the best available citation, what was implemented or studied, key findings, and completeness assessment.

## 1. Complete MES Implementations (Multiple MESA Functions)

### 1.1 Saenz de Ugarte, B., Artiba, A., & Pellerin, R. (2009). "Manufacturing execution system -- a literature review." *Production Planning & Control*, 20(6), 525--539.
- **What they studied:** Comprehensive literature review of MES evolution from mid-1990s onward; covers all MESA-11 functions and how MES functionality has expanded over time.
- **Key findings:** MES evolved from simple data-collection tools to integrated systems supporting most manufacturing execution processes from production order release to finished-goods delivery. The authors note MES still falls short in adaptive decision-making for dynamic organizations. They identify the need for better integration with supply chain replenishment strategies.
- **Completeness:** Full survey -- covers all 11 MESA functions conceptually. **Foundational review paper.**
- **Source:** [Taylor & Francis](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/09537280902938613)

### 1.2 Elliott, R. F. (2013). *Manufacturing Execution System (MES): An Examination of Implementation Strategy.* Master's thesis, California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo.
- **What they implemented:** Case study of MES deployment at a high-tech manufacturing company in California. Proposes a 14-stage systematic planning approach covering technical, software, and organizational factors.
- **Key findings:** Defines general MES concepts and inputs, recommends adaptations of the manufacturing environment, addresses SME-specific implementation challenges. Emphasizes change management and phased rollout.
- **Completeness:** Complete implementation strategy covering multiple MESA functions.
- **Source:** [Cal Poly Digital Commons](https://digitalcommons.calpoly.edu/theses/997/)

### 1.3 Govindaraju, R., & Putra, K. (2016). "A methodology for Manufacturing Execution Systems (MES) implementation." *IOP Conference Series: Materials Science and Engineering*, 114, 012094.
- **What they implemented:** MES at an Indonesian steel manufacturer. Developed a methodology using the ISA-95 reference model to define system requirements and boundaries between ERP, MES, and automation systems.
- **Key findings:** Functional integration (making all manufacturing system components work together) is identified as the most difficult challenge. The proposed methodology was refined from general IS methodologies based on lessons learned.
- **Completeness:** Complete implementation with ISA-95 alignment.
- **Source:** [IOPscience](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1757-899X/114/1/012094)

### 1.4 Contributions of MES to Improve Competitive Priorities -- Aluminum Rolling Company Case Study. *POMS Conference Proceedings*.
- **What they implemented:** Full MES deployment at a multinational aluminum rolling company. Studied organizational factors supporting MES implementation.
- **Key findings:** Hot-rolling mill received automated product data, enabling automatic equipment setup with significant error reduction. Achieved: reduction of setup time, improved information quality, increased product traceability, and reduction of nonconforming products. Organizational learning networks formed around MES deployment.
- **Completeness:** Complete multi-function implementation (scheduling, quality, traceability, resource management).
- **Source:** [POMS Proceedings](https://pomsmeetings.org/confpapers/051/051-0529.pdf)

### 1.5 Leveraging MES Integration for Operational Excellence in a Greenfield Automotive Stamping Plant: A Digital Transformation Case Study. (2026). *Scientific Engineering Journal*, 42(1).
- **What they implemented:** MES in a newly established automotive stamping facility with tandem press line, integrated during plant design stage.
- **Key findings:** OEE increased from 47.17% to 72.36% over seven months. Cumulative average changeover time decreased by 37.5%. MES checklists standardized performance across operators. Real-time data collection and standardized workflows contributed to early process stabilization.
- **Completeness:** Complete implementation -- scheduling, quality control, traceability, OEE tracking, changeover management.
- **Source:** [Scientific Engineering Journal](https://ejournals.swu.ac.th/index.php/sej/article/download/17059/13461/67634)

### 1.6 Mayer, L., Mehdiyev, N., & Fettke, P. (2020). "Manufacturing execution systems driven process analytics: A case study from individual manufacturing." *Procedia CIRP*, 97.
- **What they implemented:** Data-driven CAPP solution leveraging MES data in a tool and fixture construction firm (individual manufacturing).
- **Key findings:** Proposed a conceptual framework and architecture for MES-driven process analytics. Demonstrates how MES data can be leveraged for process mining and analytics beyond traditional MES functions.
- **Completeness:** Partial -- focuses on analytics extension of MES data, not full MES deployment.
- **Source:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2212827120314608)

### 1.7 Chen, X., Nophut, C., & Voigt, T. (2021). "A model-driven approach for engineering customizable MES with the application to the food and beverage industry." *International Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology*, 115(7--8).
- **What they implemented:** Model-driven MES engineering framework with six lifecycle phases, applied to food and beverage manufacturing.
- **Key findings:** The model-driven approach allows MES to be automatically generated and sustainably improved, reducing implementation complexity. Potentially reduces resources needed for MES customization.
- **Completeness:** Complete framework -- covers MES lifecycle from design to continuous improvement.
- **Source:** [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00170-021-07317-7)

### 1.8 Giannetti, C., et al. (2020). "Implementation of the Manufacturing Execution System in the Food and Beverage Industry." *Journal of Food Engineering* (Elsevier).
- **What they implemented:** MES implementation across food and beverage manufacturing operations.
- **Key findings:** Examines domain-specific requirements for F&B including batch tracking, hygiene compliance, and shelf-life management integrated into MES functions.
- **Completeness:** Complete implementation study in a specific industry vertical.
- **Source:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0260877420300315)

### 1.9 MES Implementation for Industry 4.0: Overcoming Buyer-Provider Information Asymmetries through Knowledge Sharing Dynamics. (2024). *Computers & Industrial Engineering* (Elsevier).
- **What they studied:** Qualitative interviews with 56 experts from 33 companies plus three buyer-provider dyad case studies on MES implementation.
- **Key findings:** Explains buyer-provider relationships during MES implementation. Knowledge sharing dynamics are critical for successful deployment. Identifies information asymmetries as a key barrier.
- **Completeness:** Multi-case study of complete MES implementations across industries.
- **Source:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0360835224006041)

## 2. Wire and Cable Industry MES

### 2.1 UL Solutions. "MES in the Cable Industry: The History and Future."
- **What they describe:** Evolution of MES in wire/cable manufacturing from simple job-list conveyance to modern systems combining workflow control with live production line and testing equipment data.
- **Key findings:** Modern cable MES injects quality control across the entire product lifecycle -- from planning to analysis of quality losses, traceability, nonconformance management, and data recording. Historical cable MES was limited to planning and WIP costing; modern systems now integrate real-time machine data.
- **Completeness:** Industry overview -- not a single implementation case study, but comprehensive description of cable-specific MES capabilities.
- **Source:** [UL Solutions](https://www.ul.com/insights/mes-cable-industry-history-and-future)

### 2.2 UL Solutions (Cimteq). CableMES -- Wire and Cable Manufacturing Execution System.
- **What it is:** Purpose-built commercial MES for wire and cable manufacturing. Provides historian tool collecting all manufacturing process data down to exact cable lengths, correlating with process/quality alarms.
- **Key capabilities:** Length-based production tracking, scrap minimization, process data historian, quality alarm correlation, trend identification, long-term benchmarking.
- **Completeness:** Commercial product -- covers multiple MESA functions tailored to cable manufacturing.
- **Source:** [CableMES](https://www.ul.com/software/cablemes-wire-and-cable-manufacturing-execution-system)

### 2.3 InnoVites. cableCORE MES.
- **What it is:** Another commercial cable-specific MES platform with industry-specific features for cable production.
- **Key capabilities:** Designed for length-based manufacturing; integrates with ERP, WMS, MRP, and PDM systems.
- **Completeness:** Commercial product.
- **Source:** [InnoVites](https://www.innovites.com/cablecore-mes)

### 2.4 UL Solutions. "Applying Industry 4.0 to the Cable Factory Environment."
- **What they describe:** Digital transformation roadmap for cable factories including MES integration with ERP, IIoT, machine vision for quality inspection, and blockchain for traceability.
- **Key findings:** All major systems (ERP, WMS, MRP, PDM, MES) have the potential to be fully integrated. MES acts as the bridge between enterprise planning and shop-floor operations in cable manufacturing.
- **Completeness:** Industry perspective paper -- describes how Industry 4.0 applies to cable manufacturing with MES as a central element.
- **Source:** [UL Solutions](https://www.ul.com/insights/applying-industry-40-cable-factory-environment)

### 2.5 UL Solutions. "MES for Cable Production: To Build or Buy?"
- **What they studied:** Decision framework for cable manufacturers choosing between acquiring an IIoT platform to build MES from scratch versus purchasing a purpose-built cable MES.
- **Key findings:** Purpose-built MES solutions embed industry-specific knowledge reducing implementation risk and time-to-value compared to generic platforms.
- **Completeness:** Decision-framework analysis.
- **Source:** [UL Solutions](https://www.ul.com/insights/mes-cable-production-build-or-buy)

**Note:** No peer-reviewed academic papers specific to wire/cable MES were found. The literature is dominated by industry white papers from UL Solutions (formerly Cimteq) and commercial vendors. ## 3. ISA-95 Implementations

### 3.1 Apiliogullari, L. (2022). "Digital transformation in project-based manufacturing: Developing the ISA-95 model for vertical integration." *International Journal of Production Economics*, 245, 108413.
- **What they implemented:** Extended ISA-95 model adding a product domain and connectivity layer for project-based manufacturers. Validated through a case study implementing vertical integration.
- **Key findings:** The standard ISA-95 model does not include all components needed for vertical integration (notably missing the product domain). The extended model helps businesses identify where to focus digital transformation efforts.
- **Completeness:** Complete ISA-95 extension with case study validation.
- **Source:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0925527322000068)

### 3.2 Panetto, H., Yahia, E., et al. (2015). "PLM Standards Modelling for Enterprise Interoperability: A Manufacturing Case Study for ERP and MES Systems Integration Based on ISA-95." *6th International IFIP Working Conference (IWEI 2015)*, Nimes, France. Springer.
- **What they implemented:** Manufacturing case study (DEKENZ scenario) for ERP-MES integration using ISA-95 standard. Created B2MML files for implementation analysis and development of B2MML converters/quality checkers.
- **Key findings:** Demonstrated model-driven methodology for PLM standards modeling and enterprise interoperability. Re-used and instantiated templates across different collaboration scenarios.
- **Completeness:** Complete ISA-95 integration case study with B2MML.
- **Source:** [Springer](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-662-47157-9_14)

### 3.3 World Batch Forum (WBF). (2008). *ISA 95 Implementation Experiences.* WBF Book Series. ISBN: 9781606502150.
- **What they compiled:** Book of examples and case studies of ISA-95 standard usage for integrating manufacturing operations with business enterprise systems -- inventory, accounting, customer relations.
- **Key findings:** Includes chapters on mapping SAP PP-PI with ISA-95, B2MML for plant maintenance and asset management, best practices workflow descriptions using B2MML, and using ISA-95 as a manufacturing enterprise analytic tool. Case study on UHT milk line implementation.
- **Completeness:** Multiple complete real-world ISA-95 implementation cases across industries.
- **Source:** [Google Books](https://books.google.com/books/about/ISA_95_Implementation_Experiences.html?id=CRHnkBLqZi4C)

### 3.4 Brandl, D. "Practical Applications of the ISA 95 Standard." MESA International / Yokogawa.
- **What they describe:** Practical guidance on applying ISA-95, including B2MML as an XML implementation created by the World Batch Forum for standardizing messages (production orders, schedules, performance results).
- **Key findings:** Automotive supplier integrated SAP ERP with MES through B2MML messages, reducing integration effort by 60%. Food manufacturer (OWS Foods) deployed Rockwell Plex aligned with ISA-95, improving forecasting accuracy by 25% and reducing customer lead times by 33%.
- **Completeness:** Multiple practical implementation examples.
- **Source:** [Yokogawa](https://web-material3.yokogawa.com/2/11821/tabs/document_11744.pdf)

## 4. AI/ML in MES

### 4.1 Shojaeinasab, A., Charter, T., Jalayer, M., Khadivi, M., Ogunfowora, O., Raiyani, N., Yaghoubi, M., & Najjaran, H. (2022). "Intelligent manufacturing execution systems: A systematic review." *Journal of Manufacturing Systems*, 62, 503--522.
- **What they studied:** Systematic literature review assessing state-of-the-art intelligent MES research and implementations. Defines five intelligence levels for MES.
- **Key findings:** Industry 4.0 requires MES that makes the shop floor autonomous, adaptable, and smart. The paper presents main MES functionalities and five intelligence levels. Uses bibliometric and network analysis to identify key institutions, authors, and countries with highest impact. First systematic literature review specifically on intelligent MES models.
- **Completeness:** Comprehensive survey -- **the definitive review paper on AI in MES.**
- **Source:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0278612522000048)

### 4.2 Mantravadi, S., Li, C., & Moller, C. (2019). "Multi-agent Manufacturing Execution System (MES): Concept, Architecture & ML Algorithm for a Smart Factory Case." *Proceedings of the 21st International Conference on Enterprise Information Systems (ICEIS 2019)*. SciTePress.
- **What they implemented:** Multi-agent MES architecture with embedded AI, combining MES system architecture with machine learning anomaly detection for autonomous production execution.
- **Key findings:** Machine learning anomaly detection algorithm integrated into multi-agent MES facilitates autonomous production execution and process optimization. MES application as enterprise information system still lacks decision support capabilities on the shop floor, which the multi-agent approach addresses.
- **Completeness:** Prototype implementation with ML integration -- partial but innovative.
- **Source:** [SciTePress](https://www.scitepress.org/Papers/2019/77689/77689.pdf)

### 4.3 Cupek, R., Ziebinski, A., Huczala, L., & Erdogan, H. (2016). "Agent-based manufacturing execution systems for short-series production scheduling." *Computers in Industry*, 82, 245--258.
- **What they implemented:** Agent-based MES architecture dedicated to short-series production support, with functional models based on ANSI/ISA-95 (IEC/ISO 62264) standard.
- **Key findings:** Agent-based approach enables flexible scheduling and execution for low-volume, high-variety production. ISA-95 standard provides the structural foundation for the agent architecture.
- **Completeness:** Complete agent-based MES architecture with ISA-95 alignment.
- **Source:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0166361516301233)

### 4.4 Peres, R. S., et al. (2023). "A Digital Twin-Based Distributed Manufacturing Execution System for Industry 4.0 with AI-Powered On-The-Fly Replanning Capabilities." *Sustainability (MDPI)*, 15(7), 6251.
- **What they implemented:** MES architecture capable of autonomously composing, verifying, interpreting, and executing production plans using digital twins and symbolic planning methods.
- **Key findings:** The architecture enables on-the-fly replanning when disruptions occur, using AI-powered symbolic planners integrated with digital twin representations of the production system.
- **Completeness:** Complete distributed MES architecture with AI and digital twin integration.
- **Source:** [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2071-1050/15/7/6251)

### 4.5 An Immersive Digital Twin Applied to a Manufacturing Execution System for the Monitoring and Control of Industry 4.0 Processes. (2024). *Applied Sciences (MDPI)*, 14(10), 4125.
- **What they implemented:** Architecture for industrial process monitoring and control using immersive digital twin integrated with MES, incorporating cyber-physical systems, robotics, IoT, AR/VR, and OPC UA.
- **Key findings:** The immersive digital twin provides enhanced operator awareness and interaction capabilities beyond traditional MES dashboards.
- **Completeness:** Complete architecture with novel immersive visualization approach.
- **Source:** [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/14/10/4125)

### 4.6 Leveraging Intelligent Manufacturing Execution Systems for Digital Transformation in the Food and Beverage Industry. (2025). *Journal of Intelligent Manufacturing and Special Equipment*, 6(2), 155. Emerald Publishing.
- **What they studied:** Evolution of MES into intelligent platforms (IMES) in the F&B industry, with eight case studies from dairy, meat, dietary supplement, brewing, and beverage industries.
- **Key findings:** IMES facilitated by AI, IoT, and big data analytics provides real-time visibility, predictive capabilities, and adaptive solutions. Examples show advancements in operational efficiency, traceability, inventory reduction, waste minimization, and process transparency. Global MES market expected to grow from $12.47B (2022) to $36.9B (2032).
- **Completeness:** Eight complete industry case studies with AI-enhanced MES.
- **Source:** [Emerald](https://www.emerald.com/jimse/article/6/2/155/1269964/Leveraging-intelligent-manufacturing-execution)

## 5. Open-Source or Academic MES

### 5.1 CSIR South Africa. (2022). "Core Functional MES with Machine Monitoring using Open-Source Software." *IEEE Conference Publication*.
- **What they implemented:** Low-cost MES based on open-source software (Node-RED core, InfluxDB, MongoDB, Tasmota) for South African SMMEs. Guided by MESA standards and definitions.
- **Key findings:** Proves that a functional MES can be built using open-source software. Provides product creation, machine monitoring, and dashboarding functions. Significantly lower cost than commercial alternatives.
- **Completeness:** Partial -- core MES functions (monitoring, dashboarding) but not all 11 MESA functions.
- **Source:** [IEEE Xplore](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9828813/)

### 5.2 de Oliveira, E. et al. (2019). "Development of an open source-based manufacturing execution system (MES): industry 4.0 enabling technology for small and medium-sized enterprises." *Scientia cum Industria*, 7(2).
- **What they implemented:** MES developed using open-source technologies targeting SMEs, with functional characteristics comparable to commercial systems.
- **Key findings:** Achieved 76.22% similarity to commercial MES functionality at a sale price up to 55.3% lower. Demonstrates viability of open-source MES for Industry 4.0 SME adoption.
- **Completeness:** Near-complete -- covers majority of standard MES functions at reduced cost.
- **Source:** [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/332594763_Development_of_an_open_source-based_manufacturing_execution_system_MES_industry_40_enabling_technology_for_small_and_medium-sized_enterprises)

### 5.3 OpenMES Project.
- **What it is:** Free, open-source MES designed to streamline production processes, enhance visibility, and drive operational excellence.
- **Source:** [OpenMES](https://www.getopenmes.com/)

### 5.4 Carbon (crbnos). Open-source ERP/MES/QMS.
- **What it is:** Open-source combined ERP, MES, and QMS for complex assembly, contract manufacturing, and configure-to-order manufacturing.
- **Source:** [GitHub](https://github.com/crbnos/carbon)

### 5.5 Smart MES (jukbot). Open-source MES for JobShop manufacturers.
- **What it is:** Smart MES web application for planning manufacturing processes, simulating workflows, tracking actual production events, and generating reports.

- **What they studied:** How Industry 4.0 dictates the end of traditional centralized MES and envisions ecosystems of smart factories with autonomous shop-floor entities.

- **Key findings:** Highlights implementation costs, integration complexities, and cybersecurity as key challenges. Underscores necessity of AI-driven MES, modular architectures, and blockchain-based security frameworks.

### 6.6 Reviewing Manufacturing Execution System in Industry 4.0: A Global Approach. (2025). *EAI Endorsed Transactions on Digital Transformation of Industrial Processes*.
- **What they studied:** Maps the MES research landscape within Industry 4.0 through SLR and bibliometric analysis. Identifies core research themes, technological trends, and persistent challenges.
- **Completeness:** Comprehensive review.
- **Source:** [EAI](https://publications.eai.eu/index.php/dtip/article/view/9768)

### 6.7 Literature Review on Challenges and Barriers for MES Implementation. (2026). *Procedia Computer Science* (Elsevier).
- **What they studied:** Comprehensive review of challenges and barriers to MES implementation across industries.
- **Key findings:** Classifies barriers into five domains: technical, organizational, human/skills-related, contextual/external, and financial. Most past barriers are readiness-related (funding, expertise, compatibility), while implementation-specific barriers are an emerging area.
- **Completeness:** Focused review on barriers.
- **Source:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1877050926001572)

### 6.8 Dutta, G., Kumar, R., Sindhwani, R., & Singh, R. K. (2022). "Overcoming the barriers of effective implementation of manufacturing execution system in pursuit of smart manufacturing in SMEs." *Procedia Computer Science*, 200, 820--832.
- **What they studied:** Five key barriers and mitigations for MES adoption in SMEs: constructive closed-loop, cross-functional integration, flexible operations orchestrations, high-availability cloud connection, and insightful data contextualization.
- **Key findings:** MES can serve as an intelligence hub integrating product lifecycle with manufacturing. Knowing barriers and mitigations in advance allows manufacturers to pre-empt challenges and make MOM/MES adoptions successful.
- **Completeness:** Focused barrier analysis for SMEs.
- **Source:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1877050922002885)

### 6.9 MES Application within Manufacturing SMEs towards KPI Development and Implementation. (2024). *Sustainability (MDPI)*, 16(7), 2974.

## 8. MESA International Publications

### 8.1 MESA International. "History of the MESA Models." mesa.org.
- **MESA-11 Model (1996):** Original model defining 11 core MES functions: (1) Resource Allocation and Status, (2) Operations/Detail Scheduling, (3) Dispatching Production Units, (4) Document Control, (5) Data Collection/Acquisition, (6) Labor Management, (7) Quality Management, (8) Process Management, (9) Maintenance Management, (10) Product Tracking and Genealogy, (11) Performance Analysis.
- **Context Model (1996):** Expanded view showing MES relationships with external enterprise systems.
- **Collaborative Model (2002):** Added collaborative manufacturing operations.
- **Strategic Initiatives Model (2008):** Spans from enterprise strategic initiatives through business operations to plant operations, showing interrelationships with objectives cascading down and results reported up.
- **Smart Manufacturing Model (current):** Newest model defining the landscape of Smart Manufacturing with eight focus areas, considering how traditional concepts become "smart" through advanced technology integration.
- **Source:** [MESA International](https://mesa.org/topics-resources/mesa-model/history-of-the-mesa-models/)

### 8.2 MESA White Paper #39: "MESA Model Evolution."
- **What it covers:** Detailed documentation of the evolution from MESA-11 through current models.

### 8.3 MESA International. "MES Explained: A High Level Vision." White Paper #6.
